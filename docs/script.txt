THE RIVER
Written by J. Panc

FADE IN:

EXT. RIVER BANK - PRE-DAWN

Fog sits low on the water. The river moves slowly, black and
cold. A single lamp post flickers at the edge of the bank,
casting orange light on wet grass.

MARA (38) stands at the water's edge in a heavy coat. She
holds a photograph but does not look at it. She looks at the
river.

The sound of water against stone. Nothing else.

                    MARA
          (barely audible)
     He used to say the river remembers
     everything you throw into it.

She crouches down. Presses her palm flat against the mud.

CUT TO:

INT. MARA'S KITCHEN - MORNING

Harsh fluorescent light. Coffee going cold on the counter.
MARA sits at the table staring at nothing. The photograph
is face-down in front of her.

A clock ticks. A radiator hisses. Outside, rain begins.

She turns the photograph over. A man in his sixties. Smiling.
She closes her eyes.

CUT TO:

EXT. BRIDGE - CONTINUOUS RAIN - DAY

MARA crosses the old stone bridge on foot. Below, the river
has swollen from the rain. It is loud now — churning, dark,
urgent.

She stops mid-bridge. Leans over the railing. Watches the
water.

A truck passes behind her. Wind catches her hair. She
doesn't move.

                    MARA (V.O.)
     There are things that take years to
     reach you. He left in November. The
     grief arrived in March.

CUT TO:

INT. COMMUNITY HALL - EVENING

Folding chairs arranged in a circle. Seven people sit in
them. MARA is one. A grief support group.

The FACILITATOR (50s, calm, unhurried) speaks. We can't hear
the words — only the sound of the rain outside, steady and
indifferent.

MARA holds her coffee with both hands. Watching the floor.

CUT TO:

EXT. RIVER BANK - NIGHT

The river again. This time, full moon. The water catches the
light and holds it.

MARA sits on a rock. She takes the photograph from her pocket.
Studies it for a long time.

Then, slowly, she folds it. Places it inside a small tin box.
Sets the box down beside her.

She doesn't throw it in. She just sits with it.

The river moves. The moon moves. She stays.

FADE TO BLACK.

EXT. FIELD BEHIND THE HOUSE - THE FOLLOWING SPRING

Mara walks through tall grass. It is not raining. The sky
is the colour of old linen. Behind her, the house. Ahead,
the tree line and beyond it, the river.

She walks without hurrying.

For the first time, she does not look afraid of where she
is going.

FADE OUT.

END OF EXCERPT
