THE RIVER — MOODBOARD & SONIC REFERENCE NOTES

VISUAL PALETTE
- Pre-dawn blue-grey before any warmth enters the frame
- Wet surfaces: dark mud, rain-soaked stone, reflective asphalt
- Lamplight orange against deep blue cold
- Moonlight on moving water — silver, fragmented, restless
- Spring green at the very end — pale, tentative, not yet fully saturated

EMOTIONAL REGISTER
Primary: Quiet devastation. Not loud grief. The kind that makes you stare at a wall.
Secondary: Muscle memory of love — reaching for someone who is no longer there
Tertiary: Acceptance as a physical process — like water finding its level

SONIC TEXTURES WE ARE DRAWN TO
- The sound of a room after everyone has left it
- Low frequency resonance of a large empty space — church, bridge, a hall in winter
- Piano with the sustain pedal held — notes bleeding into each other, no clean edges
- Strings that feel like they are being pulled slowly apart
- Rain on different surfaces — tin roof, stone, car roof, river — as rhythmic layers
- The moment between two notes of silence

WHAT THIS FILM SOUNDS LIKE TO US
Water that has been still for a very long time, then begins to move.

The music should feel like the river — it does not react to Mara. It was there before her
and will be there after. But somehow it holds her story anyway.

REFERENCE IMAGES (for texture translation into sound)
- Edward Hopper: isolated figures in large spaces, unresolved tension
- Hiroshi Sugimoto seascapes: long exposure, time collapsed into a single image
- Late winter light through bare trees — structural, cold, beautiful without being warm

THINGS TO AVOID
- Emotional signposting — we don't need the music to tell us she is sad
- Busy arrangements — every element should be load-bearing or absent
- Resolution before the final scene — do not let the music exhale before she does

THE ARC IN THREE PHRASES
1. Water: dark, cold, deep, still under the surface
2. Rain: persistent, patient, indifferent, cleansing without comfort
3. Light through grass: tentative warmth, morning, forward movement — one breath at a time
